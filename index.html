Why this is the right moment
  • The Wagner restoration wraps later this year — the relaunched site is the natural companion to that milestone, ready to tell the renovation story with new photography and content
  • Sky Valley Philharmonic has joined our programs and isn’t reflected anywhere on the current site
  • The site’s structure no longer matches how we actually work or what we offer

What we’re addressing
  • Navigation has 13+ top-level items with significant redundancy — “Donate” appears 3 times pointing to 2 different URLs, “Subscribe” appears twice, “Contact” and “Contact Us” both exist as separate entries
  • “Join Us” and “Volunteer” are separate pages with heavily overlapping purposes
  • MAC News contains only posts from 2018–2019 — we’ll convert this to a Monroe Arts Monthly newsletter archive rather than deprecating it outright
  • No clear primary CTA exists anywhere on the site, and the Accessibility Map is buried as a top-level nav item
  • The homepage represents our core programs well but doesn’t include Sky Valley Philharmonic, SCT, or other partners we should feature

What stays
  • The “Ars longa, vita brevis” quote and attribution — a strong centering statement that earns its place on the homepage
  • Our Bloomerang-embedded donation form — it works, it gives us donor management we’d otherwise need to build, and it stays exactly where it is
Already underway: Two zero-cost cleanups that don’t need to wait for the redesign — consolidating the duplicate Donate links to a single destination, and unpublishing the stale MAC News blog ahead of its conversion to a newsletter archive. These reduce visitor confusion immediately while the larger work continues.

Photography — the asset gap that needs an owner
The honest assessment Hero photography drives more first-impression credibility than any other single element on the site. SCT’s scope covers design and technical advisory; image capture is not part of their barter. We need a clear point of view and an owner before launch.

Recommended approach: in-house with volunteer support I have photography experience and equipment. Other officers and board members do as well. Treating this as a coordinated volunteer effort — with a focused shot list and a few scheduled sessions — gives us flexibility, low cost, and the right people behind the camera. Contracting it out remains a fallback if the volunteer effort stalls.

What “good” looks like — and what won’t cut it The most impactful images are well-lit performances against clean backgrounds, completed renovation interiors with intentional composition, and candid hero shots that convey energy and humanity. Boring exterior curb shots that already exist on Google Maps will not carry a visual redesign — if we use those, the redesign loses its impact regardless of how good the layout is.

Shot list priorities (proposed)
  • Wagner auditorium — full house during a performance, lighting on, audience visible
  • Wagner stage — performers in action, dramatic lighting, restoration finishes visible
  • Restored lobby and finishings — hero detail shots with intentional composition
  • Candid behind-the-scenes — volunteers setting up, board members at work, performers in rehearsal
  • Program-specific images — Monroe Community Band, Sky Performing Arts, Sky Valley Philharmonic
  • Marquee & exterior — only if captured at golden hour or during a lit evening event, never daytime curb shots

SquareSpace plan transition — an opportunity to right-size
Current: Commerce Basic at $38/month (legacy plan). Includes commerce features we don’t use — donations are handled by our embedded Bloomerang form, and we don’t sell tickets or merchandise through the site.

Proposed: SquareSpace Core at $23/month (annual billing). Unlocks the custom CSS and code injection the redesign needs to support our type system and design tokens. Includes advanced analytics and unlimited contributors.

Net effect: Saves roughly $180/year while enabling capabilities the redesign requires. Bloomerang continues to handle all donor-facing transactions exactly as it does today.

Note: switching off the legacy plan is one-way — we can’t return to Commerce Basic later. Given the legacy plan costs more and lacks the technical features we need, this is straightforward.

What comes after launch
The redesign is infrastructure for what’s next: a Phase 2 conversation about member portals, volunteer coordination, and donor account access — likely some combination of SquareSpace Member Areas and a tool like Givebutter or Bloomerang’s expanded modules. Phase 2 scoping is intentionally not part of this conversation, but flagging it so officers know it’s on the horizon.
